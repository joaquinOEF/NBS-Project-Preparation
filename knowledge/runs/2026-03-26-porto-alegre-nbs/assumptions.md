# Validated Assumptions — POA Resiliente Verde

## Run: 2026-03-26-porto-alegre-nbs

---

## Institutional & Political Assumptions

1. **CAPAG Rating**: Assumed B or better (Porto Alegre is a state capital with GDP ~R$11.5B; major municipalities typically maintain CAPAG A or B). **Must verify with STN before BNDES application.**
2. **Political support**: Assumed strong based on post-2024 flood political context (R$4B flood protection overhaul announced, PDDUA revision accelerated). Formal ofício from Prefeito not yet obtained. **Placeholder text included in concept note.**
3. **Inter-secretaria coordination**: Assumed SMAMUS will formalize a Grupo de Trabalho (GT) inter-secretarial for NBS, drawing on DMAE, SMPAE, and SMF. No existing GT confirmed.
4. **Technical team**: Assumed SMAMUS + DMAE have sufficient baseline technical capacity, supplemented by UFRGS/IPH partnership. Specific team composition TBD.

## Cost & Financial Assumptions

5. **Unit costs**: Based on literature ranges (international + Brazilian comparables), not Porto Alegre-specific engineering estimates. Applied mid-range values. USD/BRL conversion at 5.0.
6. **Municipal counterpart**: Assumed 15% (~R$37.5M) from municipal budget, potentially including federal reconstruction transfers already allocated to Porto Alegre.
7. **OPEX sustainability**: Assumed 4% of CAPEX annually (~R$10M/yr) funded through combination of municipal budget + PPP model (Orla do Guaíba precedent) + stormwater fee revenues.
8. **Prior credit history**: Assumed Porto Alegre has experience with multilateral operations (World Bank P178072 active, IDB BR-L1598 in pipeline). Specific CAPAG details and prior loan portfolio not documented.

## Technical & Environmental Assumptions

9. **Carbon sequestration**: Used conservative mid-range estimate of 5,000 tCO₂e/yr based on IPCC Tier 2 factors for Atlantic Forest restoration (5-12 tCO₂e/ha/yr) × ~500 ha effective restoration area. Actual rate will depend on species mix, soil quality, and management.
10. **Flood attenuation**: 20-40% peak flow reduction based on combined NBS literature (wetlands up to 42%, bioretention 56-89% volume, flood parks 50-90% peak). No integrated hydrological model for Porto Alegre multi-zone scenario exists yet. UFRGS/IPH should validate.
11. **Heat mortality**: Used conservative 15% reduction estimate (lower bound of Lancet/European range). Acknowledges different demographics and health infrastructure in Porto Alegre.
12. **Population at risk**: Assumed ~40% of Porto Alegre's population (~555,000 people) lives in moderate-to-high flood risk zones based on 2024 event (48% territory flooded). Exact grid-level analysis not completed.
13. **Land tenure**: Assumed >80% of intervention sites on public land (APPs, parks, road verges, conservation areas) based on user confirmation. Site-specific cadastral verification needed during feasibility phase.

## Social Assumptions

14. **Green jobs**: Mid-range estimate of 800 jobs (500 establishment + 300 permanent maintenance) based on literature rates of 10-30 direct/ha establishment and 3-8 permanent/ha, scaled conservatively.
15. **Beneficiary population**: Assumed 200,000 direct beneficiaries (residents within 500m of intervention sites across all zones) and 800,000 indirect beneficiaries (city-wide flood risk reduction, water quality, carbon).
16. **Community engagement**: Assumed Porto Alegre's Orçamento Participativo tradition and existing neighborhood associations in flood-affected areas provide foundation for participatory NBS design. No formal NBS-specific consultation conducted yet.
