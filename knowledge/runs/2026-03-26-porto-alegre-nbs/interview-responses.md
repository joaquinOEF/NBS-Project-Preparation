## Phase 2: Context & Diagnosis

- **Q5 - Territorial scope**: Multi-zone portfolio (Arroio Dilúvio corridor + low-lying flood zones + hillside morros)
- **Q6 - Problem diagnosis**: Approved as drafted — converging climate risks, record 2024 floods (5.35m, 48% territory flooded, >R$88.9B statewide damages), infrastructure failure (19/23 pumps), over-reliance on grey systems, NBS needed to complement, restore ecological buffers, build adaptive capacity
- **Q7 - Strategic objective**: "Aumentar a resiliência climática urbana de Porto Alegre através de SBN que reduzam riscos de inundação, mitiguem ilhas de calor, e sequestrem carbono, complementando a infraestrutura cinza existente"
- **Q8 - Expected results**: Approved package:
  - R1: 20-40% reduction in peak stormwater flows in target zones (10-yr storm)
  - R2: 1-3°C ambient temperature reduction in corridor zones
  - R3: 5-12 tCO₂e/ha/yr sequestration across restored areas
  - R4: 60-80% of reference biodiversity restored within 10-20 years
  - R5: 500+ green jobs created (establishment + maintenance)

---

## Phase 3: Solution Design

- **Q9 - Interventions**: All four selected:
  1. Green corridors + riparian restoration (Arroio Dilúvio zone)
  2. Flood parks + bioswales (Humaitá/Centro low-lying zones)
  3. Urban forests + tree canopy (city-wide + morros)
  4. Wetland restoration (Guaíba margins + banhados)
- **Q10 - Scale**: Large (300-1000 ha, R$150-400M)
- **Q11 - Maturity stage**: Estudo (some studies exist — Regenera Dilúvio, WB P178072, Dutch assessment, UFRGS/IPH modeling)
- **Q12 - Prior history**: Confirmed known precedents:
  - Regenera Dilúvio (planning phase)
  - World Bank P178072 (active)
  - Dutch flood expert assessment (June 2024)
  - Orla do Guaíba native species restoration (operational since 2018)
  - IDB/HOT social infrastructure recovery mapping (completed)
  - SMAMUS APP mapping (completed 2024)

## Phase 4: Benefits

- **Q13 - Benefits validation**: Approved auto-calculated estimates:
  - CO₂: 2,500-8,000 tCO₂e/yr
  - Flood attenuation: 20-40% peak flow reduction (10-yr storm)
  - Stormwater: 500K-3M m³/yr captured
