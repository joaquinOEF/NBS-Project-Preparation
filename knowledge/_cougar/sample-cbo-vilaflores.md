# Associação Cultural Vila Flores — Intervention Profile

## Organization

**Name:** Associação Cultural Vila Flores (ACVF)
**Type:** Non-profit cultural association (formalized 2014)
**Location:** Rua São Carlos / Rua Hoffmann, Floresta neighborhood, 4º Distrito, Porto Alegre, RS
**Website:** vilaflores.org
**Contact:** contato@vilaflores.org
**Founded:** 2013 (formalized 2014)

**Mission:** Coordinate cultural programming and articulate with public authorities, private sector, and society for the interests of the artistic and creative community in Porto Alegre's 4th District.

**Team:** Over 100 people across 40+ initiatives sharing the space. Core team of ~15 staff managing operations, programs, and community engagement.

**Four pillars:**
1. Art and Culture
2. Architecture and Urbanism
3. Education
4. Social and Creative Entrepreneurship

## Site

**Property:** Historic architectural complex built 1925-1928, three buildings + courtyard, 1,415 m² total area.
**Heritage status:** Listed in the Municipal Inventory of Cultural Heritage.
**Neighborhood:** Floresta / 4º Distrito — heavily affected by May 2024 floods. Area 1 on the urban density map (intensive occupation, most flood-vulnerable).
**Community:** Surrounded by low-income residential areas, creative economy workers, informal settlements.

## Intervention: Várzea Lab / Programa Palafita

**Project name:** Várzea Lab (5 laboratories for climate adaptation)
**Also known as:** Programa Palafita (launched 2026, focused on climate resilience)

**NBS type:** Multi-intervention:
- Rain gardens and bioretention in public spaces
- Community gardens with climate-resilient species
- Circular economy / waste reduction lab
- Food security lab
- Climate adaptation education and community preparedness

**Funding:** US$900,000 secured from Caixa Federal Bank's Socioenvironmental Fund (Fundo Socioambiental)

**Scale:**
- 5 laboratories across low-income communities
- Primary site: Vila Flores complex (1,415 m²) + surrounding blocks
- Extended reach: 4th District neighborhoods (Floresta, São Geraldo, Navegantes)
- Target beneficiaries: ~50,000 residents in the 4th District

**Timeline:**
- 2024: Concept development, post-flood needs assessment
- 2025: Funding secured (Caixa Federal), design phase, community consultation
- 2026: Programa Palafita launch, first 2 labs operational
- 2027-2028: Full 5-lab rollout, monitoring, scaling

**Status:** Implementation phase. Funding secured. Equipment purchasing and community workshop planning underway.

## Progress

**Completed:**
- Detailed community needs assessment post-2024 floods
- Secured US$900,000 from Caixa Federal Socioenvironmental Fund
- Architectural design for rain garden installations
- Partnership established with UFRGS (Federal University) for technical support
- Selected for ME CONTA initiative (Dutch methodology for community-driven solutions)
- 12 years of community engagement track record at the site

**In progress:**
- Equipment procurement for community enterprises affected by floods
- Workshop series on income generation and climate adaptation
- Lab 1 (food security) and Lab 2 (circular economy) setup
- Community governance structure for Programa Palafita

**Challenges:**
- Post-flood infrastructure damage still being repaired
- Community trust rebuilding after displacement
- Coordination with municipal authorities (SMAMUS, DMAE)
- Long-term maintenance funding beyond the initial grant

## Community Engagement

- **Model:** Hub-based. Vila Flores serves as the physical anchor point for community activities.
- **Governance:** ACVF board + community advisory council (being formed under Programa Palafita)
- **Participatory approach:** ME CONTA methodology (Dutch origin) — popular voting on community priorities
- **Reach:** 40+ resident initiatives, 100+ regular participants, broader reach to 4th District neighborhoods
- **Vulnerable groups:** Low-income families, waste pickers, informal workers, flood-displaced residents, elderly
