# Porto Alegre Climate Resiliency Innovation Ecosystem

## Overview
Pyxera Global mapped **50+ actors and initiatives** working on climate action in Porto Alegre for the COUGAR initiative. The ecosystem is rich in ideas and energy but weakened by fragmentation and lack of coordination.

## Key Findings
1. Efforts are scattered, with little collaboration across sectors
2. Community ownership is strongest when initiatives start locally (waste pickers, agroforestry groups)
3. Skills and capacity are uneven, limiting growth and impact
4. Funding exists but rarely reaches grassroots actors

## Ecosystem Snapshot
- **34 initiatives**: 10 government-led, 10 nonprofit-led, 7 community/cooperative-led, 6 coalition-led, 1 impact business
- **22 technical partners**: 5 universities, 7 nonprofit technical partners, 10 impact businesses/NBS startups
- **14 finance actors**: 8 corporations, 4 cooperation agreements, 1 state investment agency, 1 state fund
- **7 strategic guidelines**: 1 local, 2 state, 4 national

## High-Opportunity Sectors

### Food Systems (12 initiatives mapped)
**Keystone initiatives:**
- **Agroforestry and Community Gardens** (Citizen Governance Office) — expanded post-floods, 23 Social Assistance Reference Centers, challenges: community engagement, expansion to periphery, impact measurement
- **Várzea Lab** (Vila Flores) — 5 labs for climate adaptation in low-income communities, US$900K secured from Caixa Federal
- **Seeds of New Life** (Translab) — gardens, composting, rain gardens in peripheral areas, US$160K from Regenera RS

### Waste Management (19 initiatives mapped)
- Porto Alegre: first Brazilian city with selective collection
- 1,240 tons/day collected, only 73.6 tons recycled, 471 tons recyclable → landfill
- Financial loss: ~US$500K/month, US$6M/year
- 17 formal sorting units + 6 informal

**Keystone initiatives:**
- **Comunidades do Futuro** (CEA Bom Jesus) — most advanced sorting unit, innovation hub for energy transition, connected to city-wide sorting network, fundraising ~US$1M
- **CEASA Hub** (Hortti + Impact Hub) — 50 tons food waste/day to landfill, connecting with community kitchens
- **Creative Economy with Social Justice** (Citizen Governance Office) — circular economy, productive inclusion

## Key Constraints
1. **Governance**: No integrated crisis management, political impasses between government levels
2. **Community engagement**: Declining participation, top-down approaches
3. **Technical skills**: Uneven across organizations
4. **Finance**: Exists but doesn't reach grassroots
