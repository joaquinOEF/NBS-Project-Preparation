## High Relevance: NBS Core Projects

### REDD-PLUS Results-Based Payments for Brazil Amazon Biome (ID: FP100)
- **Funder**: Green Climate Fund | **Amount**: USD 96,452,228
- **Total budget**: USD 96,452,228
- **Instrument**: Results-based payment
- **Sector**: Land use & forestry / REDD+
- **Location**: Amazon biome
- **Status**: Under implementation | **Period**: 2019-02 – 2021-01
- **Scale**: 2,036 eligible beneficiaries (small landholders and IPLCs)
- **Key interventions**: Direct payments for forest conservation (Floresta+ M1); direct payments for recovery of Permanent Preservation Areas (Floresta+ M2); digital platform for beneficiary registration and monitoring; stakeholder engagement workshops; capacity building and gender-sensitive interventions
- **Co-benefits**: Biodiversity, income generation, gender inclusion, capacity building, territorial management
- **Lessons learned**: Accurate and accessible land tenure and environmental registration are crucial for inclusive beneficiary targeting. COVID-19 highlighted the need for flexible stakeholder engagement. Gender data gaps require innovative estimation methods.
- **Financing structure**: 77% grant (economic incentives to beneficiaries); 40% of results-based payments allocated to federal government

### Santa Catarina Natural Resources Management and Rural Poverty Reduction (ID: P043869)
- **Funder**: World Bank | **Amount**: USD 62,800,000
- **Total budget**: USD 107,500,000
- **Instrument**: Senior loan
- **Sector**: Land use & forestry / Sustainable land management
- **Location**: Santa Catarina (Center/Planalto and far West regions)
- **Status**: Completed | **Period**: 2002-04 – 2009-09
- **Scale**: 880 microcatchments (~3.6 million ha), 105,000 beneficiary farm families, 850,000 ha under conservation planning
- **Key interventions**: Participatory microcatchment planning; farmer and community training; pig waste treatment systems; riparian forest replanting; environmental teaching; pilot watershed management plans; technical assistance; management information systems
- **Co-benefits**: Air quality, biodiversity, jobs, public health, water security
- **Lessons learned**: Integrated participatory planning at microcatchment scale, combined with strong training and stakeholder engagement, can significantly strengthen rural livelihoods and environmental management. Replication depends on local institutional capacity and inclusive decision-making.
- **Financing structure**: 58.4% loan / 41.6% counterpart funding

### Reforestation with Native Species around AES-Tiete Reservoirs (ID: P096337)
- **Funder**: World Bank (BioCarbon Fund) | **Amount**: USD 0 (carbon finance)
- **Total budget**: USD 27,250,000
- **Instrument**: Results-based payment
- **Sector**: Land use & forestry / Reforestation
- **Location**: Sao Paulo state (10 hydropower reservoirs)
- **Status**: Completed | **Period**: 2010-09 – 2014-12
- **Scale**: 2,500 ha reforested with 80–126 native species
- **Key interventions**: Reforestation of 2,500 ha in APPs around 10 reservoirs; native riparian forests with high species diversity; AR-AM0010 carbon methodology pilot; environmental monitoring; pest and invasive species control
- **Co-benefits**: Biodiversity, water quality
- **Lessons learned**: Natural regeneration may not occur even in legally protected areas; active reforestation and tailored management practices are required. Results-based carbon finance can enable otherwise financially unviable ecosystem restoration.
- **Financing structure**: BioCarbon Fund carbon finance; no typical loan or grant commitment
- **Emission reductions**: 400,000 tCO2e total
