# Brazilian Domestic Financing Sources for Municipal NBS/Climate Projects

## 1. BNDES - Fundo Clima (National Climate Fund)

The Fundo Clima is Brazil's flagship climate financing instrument, created by Law 12,114/2009 under the National Policy on Climate Change. Operated by BNDES, it is the largest dedicated climate finance vehicle in the country.

### Budget
- **2025**: BRL 11.2 billion approved (BRL 8.9 billion contracted, +45% vs 2024)
- **2026**: BRL 27 billion planned (largest in history)
- European co-financing: EUR 1 billion committed (KfW EUR 500M, AFD EUR 300M, CDP EUR 200M) via Letter of Intent signed Nov 2025

### Sub-programs Relevant to NBS/Urban Climate

| Sub-program | Focus areas | Interest rate | Max term | Grace period |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Desenvolvimento Urbano Resiliente e Sustentavel** | Energy efficiency in public buildings/lighting; solid waste treatment; landfill remediation with biogas; biological treatment systems; recycling; urban parks and green areas; urban spring/river preservation; slum requalification; disaster risk management | 6.15% p.a. | 25 years | 5 years |
| **Florestas Nativas e Recursos Hidricos** | Native forest restoration; reforestation; watershed protection; water resources management | 1% p.a. | 25 years | 8 years |
| **Mobilidade Urbana** | Electric buses; collective transport; green mobility infrastructure | 6.15% p.a. | 25 years | 5 years |
| **Industria Verde** | Green industrial processes; circular economy | 6.15% p.a. | - | - |
| **Transicao Energetica** | Solar and wind (8% p.a.); other clean energy | 6.15-8% p.a. | - | - |
| **Servicos e Inovacoes Verdes** | Green services; environmental innovation | 6.15% p.a. | - | - |

### Eligibility
- Private companies, municipalities, and state governments
- Minimum financing: BRL 10 million (Florestas Nativas); varies by sub-program
- Maximum: BRL 250 million per investor per year (Florestas Nativas)
- CAPAG A or B rating required for municipal borrowers (see CAPAG section below)

### Access Pathway
- Direct application through BNDES or accredited financial agents (Banco do Nordeste, BRDE, etc.)
- Projects must align with the Annual Resource Application Plan (PAPA)
- PCVR-registered projects have prioritized access

## 2. Caixa Economica Federal (CEF)

Brazil's second-largest state-owned bank and primary housing/infrastructure lender to municipalities.

### FinBRAZEEC (Financial Instruments for Brazil Energy Efficient Cities)
- **Total program**: USD 1.324 billion
- **World Bank loan**: USD 416 million (IBRD)
- **GCF contribution**: Included in blended structure
- **CTF contribution**: Included in blended structure
- **Focus**: Municipal LED street lighting conversion; industrial energy efficiency
- **Structure**: Energy Efficiency Facility with USD 766M in sub-loans + USD 225M guarantee fund
- **Target**: 18.5 million light-points nationwide; 150+ industrial EE projects

### Pro-Cidades
- Annual municipal infrastructure investment line: BRL 1.6 billion
- Supports urban development aligned with PCVR priorities
- Covers drainage, sanitation, urban mobility, and resilience infrastructure

### Housing Programs (Minha Casa Minha Vida / Habitacao)
- Can incorporate NBS elements (green infrastructure in social housing)
- Municipalities apply through CEF's standard infrastructure credit process

## 3. FINEP (Financiadora de Estudos e Projetos)

Federal innovation financing agency under the Ministry of Science and Technology.

### Climate and Green Programs
- **2023-2025 portfolio**: BRL 12 billion in 640 energy-transition projects (6x increase from 2019-2022)
- **COP30 new lines**: BRL 460 million in three new financing lines announced Nov 2025
- **FINEP Clima**: Dedicated program for climate technology and innovation (with IDB support)

### Key Calls
- **Pro-Amazonia 2025**: BRL 150 million for biotechnology, renewable energy, water management, sustainable urban development
- **Bioeconomy and Sustainability**: BRL 60 million for private-equity funds investing in bioeconomy companies
- **Subvencao Economica (Grants)**: 11 calls totaling BRL 2.18 billion across innovation themes

### Instruments
- Reimbursable financing (loans at subsidized rates)
- Non-reimbursable grants (subvencao economica)
- Equity investments through funds

### Eligibility
- Companies, research institutions, and public entities
- Focus on innovation/technology component in climate solutions

## 4. Banco do Brasil

Largest rural credit provider in Brazil with growing sustainable finance portfolio.

### Programa ABC+ / RENOVAGRO
- Successor to the original ABC (Low-Carbon Agriculture) Plan
- Renamed to RENOVAGRO (Programa para Financiamento a Sistemas de Producao Agropecuaria Sustentaveis)
- **Focus**: No-till agriculture, degraded pasture restoration, commercial forests, crop-livestock-forestry integration, biological nitrogen fixation, animal waste treatment
- **Interest rates**: Subsidized (typically 7-8.5% p.a., below market)
- **Ticket sizes**: Up to BRL 2 million per smallholder farmer
- **International backing**: JPMorgan arranged USD 800 million sustainability-linked loan for distribution

### Relevance to NBS
- Finances agroforestry systems and forest restoration on rural properties
- Can complement urban NBS when municipalities have peri-urban agricultural areas
- Watershed-level restoration projects may qualify

## 5. Banco do Nordeste (BNB)

Regional development bank serving Northeast Brazil (operates as BNDES Automatico agent).

### Fundo Clima Automatico
- Operates as accredited agent for BNDES Fundo Clima
- Simplified process for smaller projects in the Northeast
- Same terms as BNDES Fundo Clima sub-programs

### FNE Verde (Fundo Constitucional de Financiamento do Nordeste)
- Constitutional fund for Northeast development with green earmarking
- Supports sustainable infrastructure, water resources, and climate adaptation
- Priority for semi-arid municipalities

## 6. State Development Banks

### BDMG (Banco de Desenvolvimento de Minas Gerais)
- **BDMG Verde**: Launched June 2025 for green economy transition projects
- 2024: 54.5% of BRL 3.5 billion disbursements aligned with SDGs (clean energy, jobs, urban development)
- Member of the Green Bank Network (since COP26)
- International backing: CAF USD 220 million A/B loan for powershoring and sustainability
- Relevance: Can finance NBS/climate projects in Minas Gerais municipalities

### BRDE (Banco Regional de Desenvolvimento do Extremo Sul)
- Serves Parana, Santa Catarina, Rio Grande do Sul
- Operates as BNDES agent for Fundo Clima
- Focus on southern Brazil climate resilience (post-2024 RS floods)

### BANDES (Banco de Desenvolvimento do Espirito Santo)
- State-level development bank for Espirito Santo
- Smaller scale, but can channel federal resources

## 7. Municipal Climate Funds and Instruments

### CAPAG Requirements for Municipal Borrowing
All municipalities seeking credit operations with federal guarantees must have a CAPAG (Capacidade de Pagamento) rating of A or B from the National Treasury (STN).

**Assessment criteria:**
- Indebtedness ratio
- Current savings capacity
- Liquidity position

**Rating scale:** A, A+, B, B+, C, D (only A/B qualify for guaranteed borrowing)

**Recent change (Dec 2023):** Normative Ordinance MF 1,583 introduced A+ and B+ for entities meeting additional accounting quality standards (ICF indicator).

### CEPACs (Certificados de Potencial Adicional de Construcao)
- Land value capture instrument used in Sao Paulo and other cities
- Can finance NBS as part of urban operations (operacoes urbanas consorciadas)
- Traded on stock exchange (B3)

## 8. Programa Cidades Verdes Resilientes (PCVR)

Federal coordination program (not a direct financing source, but a gateway to finance).

### Structure
- Coordinated by Ministry of Environment and Climate Change
- Jointly implemented with Ministry of Cities and Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation
- Fundo Clima allocation: BRL 10 billion earmarked for PCVR priorities
- Pro-Cidades allocation: BRL 1.6 billion/year

### Thematic Areas
1. Sustainable land use and occupation
2. Green areas and urban afforestation
3. Nature-based solutions
4. Low-carbon technologies
5. Sustainable urban mobility
6. Urban waste management

### Municipal Access
- Register via institutional commitment agreement (termo de adesao)
- Gain access to: technical support, training, methodologies, partner networks
- Priority given to: metropolitan regions, socially/climate-vulnerable municipalities
- Project Bank (Banco de Projetos) for project registration and matchmaking with funders

### 100 Model Cities Initiative
- Launched April 2025: program to structure 100 high-impact climate actions in municipalities
- Technical assistance for project preparation and financing access
