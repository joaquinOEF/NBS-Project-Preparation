# International Financing Sources for NBS/Urban Climate Projects in Brazil

## 1. Green Climate Fund (GCF)

The GCF is the world's largest dedicated climate fund. Brazil has a strong portfolio with multiple active projects.

### Brazil Portfolio (from Project Evidence spreadsheet)

|---|---|---|---|---|

| FP143 | Planting Climate Resilience in NE Rural Communities | 99.5M | 202.5M | Active |
| FP181 | CRAFT - Adaptation Technologies Fund | 45.3M | 185.3M | Active |
| FP212 | &Green Fund - Agriculture and Forest Protection | 189.4M | 984.6M | Active |
| SAP031 | Marajo Resiliente - Agroforestry systems | 9.4M | 9.9M | Active |
| SAP061 | Agroforestry in cocoa (Amazon + Atlantic Forest) | 23.1M | 31.0M | Active |

### Global Programs Active in Brazil

| Program | GCF Amount | Total | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| SnCF Global - TA Facility (FP151) | 18.5M | 28M | Subnational project preparation |
| SnCF Global - Equity (FP152) | 150M | 750M | Subnational climate investment |
| Green Guarantee Company (FP197) | 40.5M | 100.3M | Guarantee instrument |

### Instruments
- **Results-based payments**: For verified emissions reductions (e.g., REDD+)
- **Grants**: For adaptation, readiness, and project preparation
- **Concessional loans**: Below-market rates for mitigation/adaptation
- **Equity**: Through funds like SnCF Global, CRAFT
- **Guarantees**: Through GGC and similar structures

### Typical Ticket Sizes
- **Simplified Approval Process (SAP)**: Up to USD 25 million GCF contribution
- **Regular proposals**: USD 50-250 million GCF contribution
- **Micro/small proposals**: Under USD 10 million

### Access Pathway
- Through Accredited Entities (AEs): BNDES, UNDP, Conservation International, FUNBIO, World Bank
- National Designated Authority (NDA): Ministry of Finance
- Readiness grants available for proposal development (up to USD 1M/year per country)
- Project Preparation Facility: Up to USD 1.5M per application

### Brazil Country Platform
- Brazil Climate and Ecological Transformation Investment Platform (BIP)
- Developed with Bloomberg Philanthropies, GFANZ, BNDES
- Target: USD 22.8 billion in climate-aligned investments
- Priority sectors: NBS and bioeconomy; industry and mobility; energy

### Instruments
- **IBRD senior loans**: Sovereign-guaranteed, 15-25 year terms, variable spread (~1.5-2% above SOFR)
- **Policy loans (DPL/DPF)**: Budget support linked to policy reforms (e.g., Rio de Janeiro USD 150M, Ceara USD 563M)
- **Results-based payments (PforR)**: Disbursement linked to verified results
- **GEF grants**: Co-financing through Global Environment Facility
- **Carbon fund instruments**: Results-based payments for emissions reductions

### Typical Ticket Sizes
- Municipal infrastructure: USD 50-200 million
- State-level programs: USD 100-600 million
- National programs: USD 250-500 million

### Access Pathway
- Sovereign guarantee required (through Ministry of Finance/STN)
- Municipality must have CAPAG A or B
- Project preparation typically 18-24 months
- World Bank can provide technical assistance during preparation

## 3. Inter-American Development Bank (IDB)

### IDB Strategy for Brazil 2024-2027
- Aligned with flagship programs: Amazonia Forever and IDB Clima
- Focus on ecological transition and sustainable development

### Key Programs
- **IDB Clima**: Dedicated climate finance facility
- **IDB Invest**: Private-sector arm for blended finance
- **FINEP Clima**: Joint program with FINEP for climate technology and innovation in Brazil (technical cooperation BR-T1412)

### Instruments
- Sovereign-guaranteed loans (similar terms to World Bank)
- Policy-based loans
- Investment grants (through special funds)
- Technical cooperation grants (typically USD 0.2-2 million)

### Typical Ticket Sizes
- Municipal/state projects: USD 50-400 million
- Technical cooperation: USD 200K-2 million
- Private sector (IDB Invest): USD 10-200 million

## 4. Agence Francaise de Developpement (AFD)

Active in Brazil with growing portfolio, especially post-COP30.

### Key Engagements
- **Fundo Clima co-financing**: EUR 300 million sovereign loan committed (part of EUR 1B European package)
- **Porto Alegre**: Co-financier of Green Resilient Urban Regeneration project (P178072) alongside World Bank
- **NUCA Program**: Urban Climate Action Program for National Development Banks, with BNDES as implementing partner
- **AFD-World Bank Partnership**: Joint framework for Brazilian climate/urban projects

### Instruments
- Sovereign loans (concessional and near-market)
- Non-sovereign loans to development banks and utilities
- Technical assistance grants
- Blended finance structures

### Typical Ticket Sizes
- Sovereign loans: EUR 50-300 million
- Non-sovereign: EUR 20-100 million
- Technical assistance: EUR 0.5-5 million

## 5. KfW (Kreditanstalt fur Wiederaufbau)

German development bank with significant Brazil presence.

### Key Engagements
- **Fundo Clima**: EUR 500 million sovereign loan (largest European contributor in the EUR 1B package)
- **Urban Resilience Finance Programme**: Extended with ICLEI at COP30 in Belem
- **IKI (International Climate Initiative)**: German government climate program, multiple projects through KfW in Brazil

### Instruments
- Sovereign development loans
- Financial cooperation through intermediary banks
- Technical cooperation (through GIZ)

### Typical Ticket Sizes
- Sovereign loans: EUR 50-500 million
- Financial cooperation: EUR 10-50 million

## 6. Global Subnational Climate Fund (SnCF Global)

Specifically designed for subnational climate projects. GCF-backed.

### Structure
- **TA Facility (FP151)**: USD 28M total (USD 18.5M GCF grant) for project preparation
  - Up to 50 pre-feasibility studies
  - Up to 40 feasibility studies
  - Support for 40 projects to reach financial close
- **Equity Fund (FP152)**: USD 750M target fund (USD 150M GCF)
  - Investment in subnational climate projects
  - Deal size: USD 5-75 million per project
  - Sectors: Solid waste, sewage, restorative agriculture, renewable energy, energy efficiency

### Relevance to Brazilian Municipalities
- Designed to fill the gap for mid-size municipal projects
- 70% of known climate solutions are at subnational level
- Can finance NBS directly

## 9. Global Environment Facility (GEF)

### Brazil Portfolio (from spreadsheet)
- **CITinova I and II**: USD 35M GEF grants + USD 380M co-financing for sustainable cities
- **Amazon Sustainable Landscapes (Phase 1 + 2)**: USD 79.6M in grants
- **Cerrado biodiversity and fire monitoring**: Multiple grants
- **Rio Grande do Sul Biodiversity**: USD 5M grant

### Instruments
- Grants only (no loans)
- Typically USD 2-15 million per project
- Require substantial co-financing (usually 3-10x leverage)

### Access
- Through GEF Agencies: World Bank, UNDP, UNEP, Conservation International, FUNBIO, FAO
- Aligned with GEF programming strategy (currently GEF-8: 2022-2026)

## 10. Bilateral Donors and Programs

### Germany (BMZ/GIZ/IKI)
- **C40 Cities Finance Facility**: Co-funded by BMZ (see preparation-facilities.md)
- **IKI projects**: Multiple climate projects in Brazil
- **GIZ technical cooperation**: Urban development, climate adaptation

### United Kingdom (FCDO)
- **C40 CFF co-funder**: Technical assistance for city climate projects
- **UK PACT (Partnering for Accelerated Climate Transitions)**: Capacity building

### Norway
- **Amazon Fund (Fundo Amazonia)**: Managed by BNDES
  - Norway is the largest donor (>USD 1.2 billion)
  - Supports deforestation reduction, monitoring, and sustainable use
  - Grants to states, municipalities, NGOs, and research institutions

### Japan (JICA)
- Infrastructure loans for Brazilian municipalities
- Technical cooperation on disaster risk management

### European Union
- **Global Gateway**: Climate-related infrastructure investment
- Co-financing through Team Europe approach (coordinated with AFD, KfW, CDP)
