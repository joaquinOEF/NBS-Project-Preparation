# Porto Alegre - Stakeholders for NBS Projects

## Municipal Government

### SMAMUS - Secretaria Municipal do Meio Ambiente, Urbanismo e Sustentabilidade
- **Role**: Urban planning, environmental policy, PDDUA enforcement, climate action (hosts PLAC)
- **Key functions**: Environmental licensing at municipal level, urban forestry management, green area oversight, APP mapping
- **Relevance to NBS**: Primary municipal authority for environmental approvals, climate planning, and integration of NBS into urban development
- **Website**: prefeitura.poa.br/smamus
- Received commendation from Brigada Militar's Environmental Command for environmental enforcement work

### DMAE - Departamento Municipal de Agua e Esgotos
- **Role**: Municipal autarquia responsible for water supply, sewage treatment, and urban drainage (since 2017)
- **Key functions**: Water treatment plants, sewage network, stormwater management, pump stations
- **Relevance to NBS**: Critical partner for green drainage infrastructure, constructed wetlands, permeable surfaces, and integration of NBS with conventional water infrastructure
- **Budget context**: Reports indicated R$ 400 million retained in DMAE accounts amid infrastructure deterioration claims (2024)
- **Website**: prefeitura.poa.br/dmae

### DEP - Departamento de Esgotos Pluviais (dissolved)
- **Status**: Dissolved in 2017; functions absorbed by DMAE
- **Legacy**: Historically managed stormwater drainage and flood protection system
- **Relevance**: Institutional gap created by dissolution contributed to deferred maintenance of flood defenses; understanding this history is critical for NBS governance design

### SMPAE - Secretaria Municipal de Planejamento e Assuntos Estrategicos
- **Role**: Strategic municipal planning, hosts World Bank project PIU (Project Implementation Unit)
- **Relevance to NBS**: Coordinates externally-funded projects, strategic alignment with municipal priorities

### Camara Municipal de Porto Alegre
- **Role**: Legislative body (36 vereadores)
- **Relevance**: Approves PDDUA revisions, zoning changes, environmental legislation, and budget allocations
- Active in flood response oversight (CPIs, public hearings with DMAE and SMAMUS)

### Prefeitura (Mayor's Office)
- **Role**: Executive authority for municipal policy and investment
- **Relevance**: Political will for NBS adoption, prioritization of climate resilience in municipal agenda

## State Government (Rio Grande do Sul)

### FEPAM - Fundacao Estadual de Protecao Ambiental Henrique Luis Roessler
- **Role**: State environmental licensing and enforcement agency
- **Key functions**: Environmental Impact Assessments (EIA/RIMA), pollution control, water quality monitoring
- **Relevance to NBS**: Required for environmental licensing of larger NBS projects; monitors Guaiba water quality; partners with SMAMUS on air quality monitoring (5 stations in Porto Alegre)
- Issued Portaria 449/2024 updating licensing procedures post-flood

### SEMA-RS - Secretaria do Meio Ambiente e Infraestrutura
- **Role**: State-level environmental policy, manages state conservation units
- **Key functions**: Oversees Parque Estadual Delta do Jacui, state environmental fund, biodiversity policy
- **Relevance to NBS**: State policy alignment, conservation unit management adjacent to Porto Alegre, potential co-funding

### DAER-RS - Departamento Autonomo de Estradas de Rodagem
- **Role**: State road infrastructure
- **Relevance**: Road reconstruction offers opportunities to integrate NBS (bioswales, permeable pavements, green corridors)

### Defesa Civil Estadual
- **Role**: State disaster management and civil defense
- **Relevance**: Flood early warning systems, evacuation planning, post-disaster recovery coordination

## Federal Government

### ANA - Agencia Nacional de Aguas e Saneamento Basico
- **Role**: Federal water resources regulator
- **Key functions**: Water allocation, dam safety, hydrological monitoring, sanitation regulation
- **Relevance to NBS**: National water policy framework, watershed-scale planning for Guaiba basin, sanitation investment regulation

### MMA - Ministerio do Meio Ambiente e Mudanca do Clima
- **Role**: Federal environmental and climate policy
- **Key functions**: Biodiversity policy, climate change policy (PNMC), Floresta Viva restoration program
- **Relevance to NBS**: National NBS policy frameworks, restoration targets, access to federal environmental funds
- Floresta Viva mobilized BRL 500+ million for restoration across all biomes

### IBAMA - Instituto Brasileiro do Meio Ambiente e dos Recursos Naturais Renovaveis
- **Role**: Federal environmental enforcement
- **Relevance**: Licensing for projects affecting federal conservation areas, enforcement of Codigo Florestal

### MCidades / MDR - Ministerio das Cidades
- **Role**: Federal urban development and housing policy
- **Relevance**: National urban resilience programs, housing reconstruction post-flood, Minha Casa Minha Vida integration

### BNDES - Banco Nacional de Desenvolvimento Economico e Social
- **Role**: National development bank
- **Relevance**: Major potential funder for NBS infrastructure; green finance products; post-flood reconstruction credit lines

## Academia and Research

### UFRGS - Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul
- **IPH (Instituto de Pesquisas Hidraulicas)**:
  - Premier water research institute in Latin America, founded 1953
  - Expertise: hydraulics, water resources, environmental sanitation, flood modeling
  - Graduate Program in Water Resources and Environmental Sanitation (Master's and Doctorate)
  - International collaborations across all continents
  - Partnered with DMAE on Porto Alegre's PMSB
  - Key resource for NBS hydrological modeling and monitoring
- **Departamento de Ecologia / Instituto de Biociencias**: Biodiversity expertise, Atlantic Forest ecology
- **Faculdade de Arquitetura**: Urban planning, landscape architecture for NBS design

### PUCRS - Pontificia Universidade Catolica do Rio Grande do Sul
- **Tecnopuc**: Technology park with environmental technology capacity
- Research groups in environmental science, urban sustainability
- Potential partner for NBS monitoring technology and data analytics

### UNISINOS (metropolitan area)
- Environmental research programs
- Located in Sao Leopoldo (Sinos River basin), relevant to watershed-scale NBS

## International Organizations

### World Bank
- **Project P178072**: Urban resilience project for Porto Alegre, targeting Centro Historico and flood defense
- Provided emergency support after May 2024 floods
- Technical assistance on NBS integration into master plan
- Guideline on "Assessing Benefits and Costs of NBS for Climate Resilience" (2023)

### IDB - Inter-American Development Bank
- **Project BR-L1598**: "Porto Alegre+" - Social Development with Fiscal Sustainability
- Building Resilience program for RS
- Technical advisory on flood risk management and NBS

### AIIB - Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank
- Recovery and Trade-Facilitation Project in Guaiba, RS (approved 2025)
- Potential co-financier for green infrastructure components

### UNDRR - UN Office for Disaster Risk Reduction
- Flood management collaboration with RS for resilient recovery
- International Recovery Platform engagement
- Technical knowledge on NBS for disaster risk reduction

### WMO - World Meteorological Organization
- Partnership with RS on flood forecasting and early warning systems

### WRI Brasil
- Brazilian office of World Resources Institute, based in Sao Paulo and Porto Alegre
- Expertise in NBS, urban forests, climate adaptation
- Potential technical partner for NBS assessment and monitoring

### ICLEI South America
- Headquartered in Sao Paulo
- Supported Porto Alegre's GHG Emissions Inventory
- Network for peer learning on local climate action and NBS

### C40 Cities
- Porto Alegre is not a C40 member but engages with C40 knowledge products
- C40's NBS guidelines and case studies are relevant references

### GIZ (Deutsche Gesellschaft fur Internationale Zusammenarbeit)
- Active in Brazilian urban climate projects
- Potential technical cooperation partner for NBS

## Civil Society

### Forum de Entidades
- Coalition of civil society organizations active in PDDUA revision process
- Advocates for stronger environmental protections, community participation, and social equity in urban planning

### Movimentos Sociais (Social Movements)
- Strong tradition of participatory governance in Porto Alegre (Orcamento Participativo since 1989)
- Community organizations in flood-affected neighborhoods (Humaita, Navegantes, Ilhas)
- Essential for inclusive NBS design and community-based monitoring

### Environmental NGOs
- **Ingra (Instituto Gaúcho de Estudos Ambientais)**: Local environmental advocacy
- **SOS Mata Atlantica**: Atlantic Forest conservation and restoration
- **WWF-Brasil**: Biodiversity and ecosystem conservation

## Private Sector

### Utilities and Concessionaires
- **CEEE Equatorial**: Electricity distribution (subject of CPI investigation at Camara)
- **CORSAN/Aegea**: Water and sanitation in metropolitan municipalities
- Private companies involved in flood reconstruction contracts

### Construction and Engineering
- Firms involved in dike reconstruction, pump station rehabilitation
- Opportunity to integrate NBS into conventional infrastructure contracts

### Technology Companies
- Porto Alegre's tech sector (Tecnopuc, Porto Digital) offers capacity for NBS monitoring, IoT sensors, data platforms
- HOT (Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team) conducted post-flood mapping for resilience

### Real Estate Developers
- Key stakeholders in PDDUA revision discussions
- Tension between development density and green space preservation
- Potential for NBS integration requirements in new developments (green roofs, permeable surfaces)
