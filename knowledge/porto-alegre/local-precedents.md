# Porto Alegre - Local NBS Precedents and Green Infrastructure

## Major Urban Parks

### Parque Marinha do Brasil
- **Area**: 67.38 hectares
- **Opened**: 1978
- **Location**: Guaiba lakefront, near Centro and Menino Deus neighborhoods
- **Features**: Largest urban park in Porto Alegre; sports facilities, lakes, cycling paths
- **NBS relevance**: Large green area providing urban cooling, stormwater absorption, biodiversity habitat, and recreation co-benefits
- **Challenges**: Partially flooded during May 2024 event; post-flood restoration needed

### Parque Farroupilha (Redencao)
- **Area**: 37 hectares
- **Opened**: 1935 (oldest public park in the city)
- **Location**: Central Porto Alegre, near UFRGS campus
- **Features**: Historic park, cultural events, Brique da Redencao (Sunday fair), mature tree canopy
- **NBS relevance**: Demonstrates long-term value of urban green space; significant carbon stock in mature trees; urban heat island mitigation

### Parque Moinhos de Vento (Parcao)
- **Area**: 11.5 hectares
- **Opened**: 1972
- **Location**: Moinhos de Vento neighborhood (high-income residential)
- **Features**: Lake, playground, sports courts, well-maintained landscaping
- **NBS relevance**: Example of high-quality urban green space with strong community ownership; demonstrates property value co-benefits

### Jardim Botanico de Porto Alegre
- **Area**: ~39 hectares
- **Location**: Jardim Botanico neighborhood
- **Features**: Native species collections, Atlantic Forest remnants, environmental education programs
- **NBS relevance**: Seed bank and native species expertise for restoration projects; demonstration of Atlantic Forest ecosystem services

## Orla do Guaiba Waterfront Park

### Project Overview
- **Design**: Jaime Lerner Arquitetos Associados
- **Phase 1 area**: 5.7 hectares over 1.5 km of waterfront
- **Opened**: 2018
- **Investment**: Part of the Guaiba waterfront revitalization contemplated in the PDDUA

### Environmental Design
- Landscape design is mindful of riparian habitat ecological aspects
- Seeks to **reintroduce native species** of the riparian environment to promote regeneration
- Planting proposal differentiates **five vegetation zones** between water and street levels, defined by existing topography
- **100% native plant species** selected for the planting palette
- Functions as both environmental regeneration and permanent environmental education

### Urban Regeneration Impact
- Described as an "urban and environmental regeneration project"
- Systemic social, economic, and environmental impacts
- Transformed Porto Alegre's relationship with its waterfront
- Managed by Gam3 Parks through public-private partnership model

### NBS Lessons
- Demonstrates that high-quality NBS can achieve both ecological restoration and public space goals
- Native species approach provides replicable model for riparian restoration elsewhere in the city
- PPP model shows viable financing pathway for NBS implementation
- Phase 1 success builds political support for expanded waterfront naturalization

## Delta do Jacui Conservation

### Parque Estadual Delta do Jacui (PEDJ)
- **Created**: 1976
- **Area**: ~17,245 hectares
- **Jurisdiction**: State (SEMA-RS)
- **Municipalities**: Porto Alegre, Canoas, Nova Santa Rita, Triunfo, Charqueadas, Eldorado do Sul
- **Protection level**: Full protection conservation unit (SNUC)

### Ecological Functions
- Maintains critical green area near the most urbanized part of the state capital
- Delta waters act as **natural filter**, contributing to Guaiba water potability
- Supports **fish productivity** and aquatic biodiversity
- Floodplain and wetland ecosystems provide natural flood attenuation
- Ecological corridor connecting Jacui, Sinos, and Gravatai river systems with Guaiba

### NBS Relevance
- Largest existing NBS asset adjacent to Porto Alegre
- Demonstrates the economic value of wetland ecosystem services (water filtration, flood control, fisheries)
- Buffer zone management offers opportunities for complementary NBS interventions
- Restoration of degraded areas within the park can enhance flood buffering capacity

## Reserva Biologica do Lami Jose Lutzenberger

### Overview
- **Area**: 179.78 hectares
- **Created**: 1975
- **Location**: Lami neighborhood, far south of Porto Alegre
- **Management**: Municipal (SMAMUS)

### Ecosystem
- Preserves banhado (wetland) ecosystems along the Guaiba southern shore
- Transition zone between Atlantic Forest and Pampa biomes
- Critical habitat for native species
- Named after Jose Lutzenberger, iconic Brazilian environmentalist from Porto Alegre

### NBS Relevance
- Model for urban wetland conservation
- Demonstrates long-term viability of municipal conservation unit management
- Reference ecosystem for banhado restoration projects elsewhere in the city

## Post-2024 Flood Green Infrastructure Projects

### Regenera Diluvio
- **Focus**: Restoration of Arroio Diluvio corridor
- **Location**: Runs through central Porto Alegre along Avenida Ipiranga
- **Approach**: Riparian buffer restoration, biofiltration, constructed wetlands
- **Partners**: Prefeitura, UFRGS, potential World Bank support
- **Status**: Planning phase; accelerated post-2024 floods
- **NBS elements**: Green corridor creation, water quality improvement, urban biodiversity enhancement

### World Bank Resilience Project (P178072)
- Targets Centro Historico and flood defense system
- Includes NBS integration components alongside grey infrastructure
- Data-driven tools for climate risk mapping
- Potential for bioretention and green infrastructure in downtown reconstruction

### Dutch Expert Collaboration on Flood Protection
- Netherlands delegation visited Porto Alegre in June 2024 at DMAE's request
- Technical assessment of flood protection system with recommendations for reinforcement
- Focus on integrating nature-based approaches with engineered flood defenses
- Knowledge transfer on Dutch water management expertise (room for the river, green-grey hybrid approaches)

### IDB/HOT Social Infrastructure Recovery Mapping
- June-July 2024 collaboration between LAC Hub, IDB, and Porto Alegre municipal departments
- Georeferenced assessment of 420+ public facilities damaged or destroyed
- Health care network: 36 facility rehabilitations and constructions planned
- Social protection network: 23 facility rehabilitations and constructions planned
- Participatory cartographic monitoring campaign for tracking recovery

### Nature-Based Flood Mitigation Proposals
- Wetland restoration in low-lying areas for flood attenuation
- Permeable surface requirements in new developments
- Green roofs and rain gardens in commercial and residential buildings
- Restoration of banhados along tributary streams
- Native vegetation restoration in upstream watersheds (ecologist Valerio Pillar: "it could reduce the damage" even if it cannot avert flooding entirely)

## Urban Reforestation Initiatives

### Municipal Tree Planting Programs
- SMAMUS manages urban forestry operations
- Afforestation Master Plan (2006) guides planting policy
- Target: 40% plant coverage on public sidewalks
- Approximately 1 million trees currently in the municipality
- Focus on native species from Atlantic Forest and Pampa biomes

### Community-Led Initiatives
- Porto Alegre's participatory budgeting tradition enables community-driven green projects
- Neighborhood associations in flood-affected areas advocating for green reconstruction
- Environmental education programs linked to Jardim Botanico and Orla do Guaiba

## DMAE Water Infrastructure Projects

### Sewage Treatment Expansion
- Ongoing effort to increase sewage treatment coverage
- Separation of combined sewer systems in priority areas
- NBS opportunity: constructed wetlands as tertiary treatment or CSO management

### Stormwater Management
- Since absorbing DEP functions (2017), DMAE manages stormwater drainage
- Post-flood focus on integrating green and grey drainage solutions
- Bioswales and retention areas being considered for new development areas

## Lessons from Local Precedents

1. **Native species work**: Orla do Guaiba demonstrates successful 100% native species approach for urban NBS
2. **PPP model viable**: Public-private partnership financing proven for green infrastructure
3. **Long-term green assets**: Parks like Redencao (90+ years) show NBS provides enduring value
4. **Conservation unit effectiveness**: Delta do Jacui's water filtration and flood attenuation services justify protection costs
5. **Institutional continuity matters**: DEP dissolution created a gap that weakened drainage management - NBS governance design must ensure institutional permanence
6. **Community engagement essential**: Porto Alegre's participatory tradition is a strength for NBS co-design
7. **Post-disaster window**: The 2024 floods created political momentum for green infrastructure investment that should be capitalized on before attention fades
