# Porto Alegre - Existing Plans and Commitments

## Master Plan (PDDUA)

### Original PDDUA (LC 434/1999)
- **Full name**: Plano Diretor de Desenvolvimento Urbano Ambiental (PDDUA)
- **Enacted**: Lei Complementar no 434, December 1, 1999
- **Major amendments**: LC 646/2010, LC 667/2011
- Establishes urban-environmental zoning, land use parameters, and environmental protection areas
- Integrates environmental sustainability into urban planning through the concept of "desenvolvimento urbano ambiental"
- Defines Special Areas of Environmental Interest (AEIAs) and flood-prone zones
- Managed by SMAMUS (Secretaria Municipal do Meio Ambiente, Urbanismo e Sustentabilidade)

### PDDUA Revision (2024-2026)
- A comprehensive revision process began after the May 2024 floods exposed critical gaps in climate adaptation
- The revised PDDUA and the companion Lei de Uso e Ocupacao do Solo (LUOS) were approved by the Camara Municipal in November 2025 (37 votes to 14)
- Opposition amendments focused on stronger environmental guidelines and sustainability provisions were largely rejected, generating controversy
- Civil society forums (including the Forum de Entidades) criticized the revision for contradictions between sustainability rhetoric and actual provisions
- Key tension: balancing real estate development pressure with flood risk management and green infrastructure preservation
- As of March 2026, the Camara continues deliberating final amendments

## Climate Action Plans

### Plano de Acao Climatica de Porto Alegre (PLAC)
- Hosted by SMAMUS
- Porto Alegre's second GHG Emissions Inventory was completed in 2021, covering 2016-2019 data
- Total emissions declined from 2.5 to 2.3 million tCO2e between 2016-2019 (5.6% reduction)
- Transport sector dominates at 67.7% of emissions (2019), followed by stationary energy (23%) and waste (8.8%)
- AFOLU (Agriculture, Forestry and Land Use) accounts for only 0.5%

### Plano Municipal de Mudancas Climaticas
- Aligns with Brazil's national climate policy (PNMC - Lei 12.187/2009)
- Sets municipal mitigation and adaptation targets
- Post-2024 floods accelerated integration of climate adaptation into urban planning frameworks

## Sanitation and Water Management Plans

### Plano Municipal de Saneamento Basico (PMSB)
- Required under Lei Federal 11.445/2007 (Marco Legal do Saneamento)
- Covers four axes: water supply, sewage treatment, stormwater drainage, and solid waste
- Prepared by DMAE in partnership with UFRGS
- Presents diagnosis, objectives, goals, and actions for universal service coverage
- Porto Alegre does not have a separate Municipal Basic Sanitation Policy (politica) but does have the operational plan
- Critical gap identified post-2024: stormwater drainage component was underfunded after DEP dissolution in 2017

### Plano de Drenagem Urbana
- Historically managed by DEP (Departamento de Esgotos Pluviais), dissolved in 2017 with functions transferred to DMAE
- The institutional disruption weakened drainage planning and maintenance capacity
- Post-flood assessments identified the need for a new integrated flood and drainage master plan
- World Bank, IDB, and UN agencies partnered with RS state to develop a state-wide flood and drainage master plan

## Multi-Year Budget Plan (PPA)

### PPA 2022-2025
- Municipal multi-year plan establishing budget priorities across government programs
- Limited dedicated climate adaptation funding prior to 2024 floods

### PPA 2026-2029 (in preparation)
- Expected to incorporate significant flood reconstruction and resilience investments
- IDB project "Porto Alegre+" (BR-L1598) aims to support social development with fiscal sustainability

## International Climate Commitments

### C40 Cities Network
- Porto Alegre is not currently a C40 member city (as of 2026)
- However, the city has engaged with C40 knowledge resources and events, particularly post-flood
- The C40 World Mayors Summit 2025 in Rio de Janeiro included RS flood lessons

### ICLEI South America
- Porto Alegre has historically engaged with ICLEI (headquartered in Sao Paulo for South America)
- ICLEI supported Porto Alegre's second GHG Emissions Inventory
- ICLEI networks facilitate peer learning on climate adaptation and NBS

### Race to Zero / Net Zero
- Porto Alegre has not formally joined the Race to Zero campaign
- Brazil's updated NDC (2025) includes a 59-67% emissions reduction target by 2035 (from 2005 levels)
- Brazil's NDC 3.0 introduces "climate federalism" recognizing the role of all 5,570 municipalities

### COP30 Alignment (Belem, 2025)
- COP30 in Belem highlighted Brazilian sub-national climate action
- RS flood experience featured prominently in discussions about loss and damage, adaptation finance

## Post-2024 Flood Reconstruction Plans

### Federal Response
- Federal government allocated emergency reconstruction funds for RS
- Prevention and disaster budget: R$ 1.9 billion (2024), reduced to R$ 1.7 billion (2025)
- Total estimated impact of RS floods: R$ 88.9 billion (~US$ 15 billion), of which 46% damages, 38% losses, 15% additional costs

### Flood Protection System Overhaul
- Estimated R$ 4 billion to fully revamp Porto Alegre's flood protection system
- R$ 400 million for pump house repairs alone
- Ongoing O&M would require R$ 200 million/year after rehabilitation
- The system comprises 68 km of dikes and barriers along the Guaiba and Gravatai banks
- Priority actions: dike repair, floodgate inspection, alert system improvement

### Multilateral Support
- **World Bank**: Emergency support and project P178072 for resilience
- **IDB**: Building Resilience program, Porto Alegre+ (BR-L1598)
- **UNDRR/WMO**: Flood management collaboration for resilient recovery
- **AIIB**: Recovery and Trade-Facilitation Project in Guaiba, RS (approved 2025)

### Recovery Strategy
- Focus on "recovery readiness" - embedding resilience into governance before future events
- Integration of NBS and data-driven tools into the revised master plan
- Community participation prioritized through existing participatory budgeting legacy
- Nature-based flood mitigation (wetland restoration, permeable surfaces) proposed alongside grey infrastructure repairs
