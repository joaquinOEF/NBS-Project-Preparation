Porto Alegre is the capital and largest city of the state of Rio Grande do Sul, and the tenth most populous city in Brazil. It is the southernmost state capital in the country.

## Geography

### Location and Topography
- Located on the eastern bank of Lago Guaiba (Guaiba Lake/River), at approximately 30.03S, 51.23W
- Topography punctuated by **40 hills (morros)**, with elevations ranging from near sea level along the waterfront to approximately 311 m at Morro Santana
- Coastal lowlands along the Guaiba and river floodplains interspersed with granite ridges
- An archipelago of islands in the Guaiba faces the city (Arquipelago neighborhood), forming a unique freshwater ecosystem

### Hydrography
- **Lago Guaiba**: Major water body (496 km2 total surface area) commonly referred to as both a river and a lake. It is where five rivers converge before flowing into Lagoa dos Patos
- **Tributary rivers**: Jacui, Sinos, Gravatai, Cai, and Taquari all feed into the Guaiba
- **Arroio Diluvio**: Major urban stream running through the city center along Avenida Ipiranga, heavily channelized and polluted
- **Lagoa dos Patos**: Giant freshwater lagoon to the south, one of the largest in the world

### Biomes
Porto Alegre sits at the **transition zone between two biomes**:
- **Mata Atlantica (Atlantic Forest)**: Remnant forest patches on the city's hills and ridges. The city area concentrates 28% of the native flora of Rio Grande do Sul, with approximately 9,288 species
- **Pampa**: Grassland biome characteristic of the southern lowlands of RS

This biome transition gives Porto Alegre exceptional biodiversity for an urban area and is highly relevant for NBS project design (native species selection, ecological corridor planning).

### Climate
- **Koppen classification**: Cfa (Humid subtropical)
- **Mean annual temperature**: 19.5C
- **Annual rainfall**: ~1,300 mm, distributed throughout the year
- Distinct four seasons; winters can be cold (occasional frost), summers hot and humid
- Subject to strong El Nino / La Nina influence on precipitation patterns

## Governance Structure

### Municipal Government (Prefeitura)
Porto Alegre is governed by a mayor (Prefeito) and a Municipal Chamber (Camara de Vereadores) with 36 seats.

**Key Secretarias relevant to NBS/climate**:
- **SMAMUS** - Secretaria Municipal do Meio Ambiente, Urbanismo e Sustentabilidade: Responsible for urban planning, environmental policy, PDDUA enforcement, and climate action (hosts the Climate Action Plan - PLAC)
- **DMAE** - Departamento Municipal de Agua e Esgotos: Water supply, sewage treatment, and (since 2017) urban drainage
- **DEP** - Departamento de Esgotos Pluviais: Historically responsible for stormwater management; **dissolved in 2017** and functions absorbed by DMAE (this institutional gap contributed to the 2024 flood failures)
- **SMPAE** - Secretaria Municipal de Planejamento e Assuntos Estrategicos: Strategic planning, hosts the World Bank project PIU
- **SMF** - Secretaria Municipal da Fazenda: Municipal finance

### Administrative Divisions
- **8 Planning Management Regions (RGPs)**: Official planning subdivisions
- **17 Participatory Budget Regions (ROPs)**: Legacy of Porto Alegre's internationally recognized participatory budgeting process (Orcamento Participativo), pioneered in 1989
- **94 official neighborhoods (bairros)**

## Economic Profile

### GDP and Structure
- **Municipal GDP**: ~R$ 11.5 billion (2024)
- The city contributes roughly half of Rio Grande do Sul's total GDP
- **Economic structure**: Services-dominated, followed by industry and agriculture

### Key Sectors
- **Services**: Education, healthcare, technology/IT, financial services, tourism
- **Industry**: Petrochemicals, automotive manufacturing, leather/footwear
- **Agriculture**: Tobacco, livestock (in broader metropolitan region)
- **Exports (2024)**: $1.35 billion - top products: soybeans ($866M), soybean meal ($187M), motor vehicles ($64.5M), rice ($61.6M), frozen beef ($43.4M)
- **Imports (2024)**: $694 million

### Notable Economic Features
- Headquarters of major companies and banks
- Major technology hub in southern Brazil (Tecnopuc at PUCRS, Porto Digital)
- Strong university-industry linkages (UFRGS, PUCRS, UNISINOS in metro area)
- Pioneer of **participatory budgeting** (Orcamento Participativo), internationally recognized governance innovation since 1989

## Key Neighborhoods and Areas Relevant to NBS

- **Centro Historico**: Downtown area protected by Muro da Maua; heavily impacted in 2024 floods; target of World Bank P178072 project
- **Arquipelago (Ilhas)**: Island neighborhoods in the Guaiba; extremely flood-vulnerable; unique ecosystem
- **Humaita-Navegantes**: Low-lying industrial/residential area; among the worst-hit in 2024
- **Restinga**: Large peripheral neighborhood; social vulnerability; significant remaining green areas
- **Lomba do Pinheiro**: Hillside peri-urban area; landslide risk; Morro Santana ecological reserve
- **Lami**: Far south; ecological reserve (Reserva do Lami); Guaiba shoreline; Pampa biome transition
- **Ipanema/Tristeza**: Southern waterfront; Guaiba beaches; moderate flood risk
- **Bairros along Arroio Diluvio** (Partenon, Petropolis, Azenha): Target of Regenera Diluvio project
