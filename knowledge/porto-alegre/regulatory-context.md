# Porto Alegre - Regulatory Context for NBS

## Municipal Legislation

### PDDUA - Plano Diretor de Desenvolvimento Urbano Ambiental
- **Law**: Lei Complementar no 434/1999
- **Major amendments**: LC 646/2010, LC 667/2011
- Establishes Porto Alegre's urban-environmental development framework
- Defines land use zoning, building parameters, and environmental protection areas
- Key environmental provisions:
  - Special Areas of Environmental Interest (AEIAs)
  - Flood-prone zone designations
  - Green area requirements for new developments
  - Integration of environmental sustainability into urban development (the "urbano ambiental" concept)
- **Current status**: Under comprehensive revision (2024-2026); revised version approved November 2025 with ongoing amendments in Camara Municipal. As of March 23, 2026, Camara rejected four opposition amendments focused on environmental directives and sustainability -- environmental provisions remain the main point of friction between opposition and government base.
- **NBS relevance**: The PDDUA defines where and how NBS can be implemented; revision is an opportunity to strengthen NBS-enabling provisions but political dynamics indicate risk of weakened environmental language

### LUOS - Lei de Uso e Ocupacao do Solo
- Companion legislation to the PDDUA regulating specific land use and building parameters
- Being revised simultaneously with the PDDUA
- Defines floor-area ratios, setbacks, and occupancy rates that affect green space availability
- Can mandate permeable surface percentages, green roof requirements, and tree planting obligations

### Municipal Environmental Licensing
- SMAMUS handles environmental licensing for projects within municipal jurisdiction
- Smaller projects (below state thresholds) are licensed directly by the municipality
- Environmental Impact Studies (EIA/RIMA) required for projects with significant environmental impact
- SMAMUS manages APP mapping and environmental permitting for urban interventions

### Afforestation Master Plan (Plano Diretor de Arborizacao, 2006)
- Establishes parameters for urban tree planting, preservation, and management
- Target: 40% plant coverage on public sidewalks
- Guides species selection and planting policies
- Porto Alegre has approximately 1 million trees; 76.5% of households have at least one tree on their street

## State Legislation (Rio Grande do Sul)

### FEPAM Environmental Licensing
- **Agency**: Fundacao Estadual de Protecao Ambiental Henrique Luis Roessler
- Handles state-level environmental licensing for larger projects
- Issues operating licenses (Licenca Previa, Licenca de Instalacao, Licenca de Operacao)
- Partners with municipalities on air quality monitoring (5 stations in Porto Alegre with SMAMUS, UFRGS, Petrobras)
- **Portaria FEPAM 449/2024**: Updated licensing procedures following the May 2024 floods
- **NBS relevance**: Larger NBS projects (wetland construction, major reforestation) may require FEPAM licensing; understanding the licensing pathway is critical for project timelines

### State Environmental Code
- Rio Grande do Sul has state-level environmental legislation complementing federal law
- State water resources policy governs Guaiba basin water use and protection
- State biodiversity policy addresses Atlantic Forest and Pampa biome conservation

### SEMA-RS Conservation Units
- Manages state conservation units including Parque Estadual Delta do Jacui
- Conservation unit buffer zones affect NBS project siting near protected areas
- SNUC (Sistema Nacional de Unidades de Conservacao) categories apply

## Federal Legislation

### Codigo Florestal (Lei 12.651/2012)
- Brazil's Forest Code establishes nationwide rules for vegetation protection
- **Key provisions for urban NBS**:

#### Areas de Preservacao Permanente (APPs)
- Legally protected areas with environmental functions (water resource protection, slope stability, biodiversity)
- **Applies to both rural AND urban areas** (clarified in Article 4 of the 2012 code, resolving prior ambiguity)
- Urban APP categories relevant to Porto Alegre:
  - **Riparian buffers**: 30m minimum along watercourses up to 10m wide; wider buffers for larger watercourses
  - **Lake/reservoir margins**: Buffer widths vary by water body size and urban/rural classification
  - **Hillside areas**: Slopes exceeding 45 degrees
  - **Hilltops and ridgelines**: Protected from development
- Porto Alegre's SMAMUS contracted specialized consulting to map and characterize all municipal APPs (completed 2024)
- **NBS opportunity**: APPs are ideal locations for ecological restoration, riparian reforestation, and green corridor projects - they are already legally protected from development

#### Legal Reserve (Reserva Legal)
- Not typically required for urban properties in consolidated urban areas
- Relevant for peri-urban and rural fringe areas of the municipality (e.g., Lami, Restinga, Lomba do Pinheiro)

#### Cadastro Ambiental Rural (CAR)
- Required for rural properties; relevant for peri-urban areas
- Registration in the environmental registry is a prerequisite for accessing rural credit and restoration programs

### PNMC - Politica Nacional sobre Mudanca do Clima (Lei 12.187/2009)
- National climate change policy framework
- Establishes emission reduction targets and adaptation measures
- Municipal climate plans (like Porto Alegre's PLAC) align with PNMC
- Brazil's NDC (updated 2025): 59-67% emission reduction by 2035 from 2005 levels

### Marco Legal do Saneamento (Lei 14.026/2020)
- Updated national sanitation framework
- Requires universalization of water supply and sewage treatment by 2033
- Mandates municipal sanitation plans (PMSB)
- Relevant to NBS integration with drainage and sewage infrastructure (green infrastructure as part of sanitation solutions)

### Lei da Mata Atlantica (Lei 11.428/2006)
- Specific protection for Atlantic Forest biome
- Restricts clearing of native vegetation in Atlantic Forest remnants
- Porto Alegre sits in the Atlantic Forest domain, making this law applicable to all forest remnants on the city's hills
- Requires environmental impact assessment for any intervention in Atlantic Forest areas
- **NBS opportunity**: Restoration of Atlantic Forest remnants qualifies for regulatory compliance and access to restoration funding

### Estatuto da Cidade (Lei 10.257/2001)
- Framework law for urban development policy
- Mandates municipal master plans (which Porto Alegre has as PDDUA)
- Provides tools for NBS integration:
  - **Transfer of Development Rights (TDR)**: Can protect green areas by compensating landowners
  - **IPTU Progressivo**: Progressive property tax on vacant/underutilized land
  - **Outorga Onerosa**: Charges for building rights above baseline, revenues can fund NBS
  - **Zonas Especiais de Interesse Social (ZEIS)**: Special zones for social housing with environmental requirements

## Environmental Fund

### Fundo Municipal do Meio Ambiente (FMMA)
- Municipal environmental fund managed by SMAMUS
- Receives revenues from environmental fines, compensation payments, and budgetary allocations
- Can finance NBS projects, environmental restoration, and conservation activities
- Post-2024 flood context: potential channel for directing reconstruction funds toward green infrastructure

### FNMA - Fundo Nacional do Meio Ambiente
- Federal environmental fund administered by MMA
- Provides grants for environmental projects including urban NBS and restoration

## Post-Flood Regulatory Changes (2024-2025)

### Federal Emergency Measures
- Federal government issued emergency decrees and medidas provisorias for RS flood response
- Expedited environmental licensing for reconstruction activities
- Risk: expedited processes may bypass environmental safeguards needed for NBS integration

### State-Level Responses
- FEPAM Portaria 449/2024 updated licensing procedures
- State government initiated flood and drainage master plan development
- Integration of climate resilience requirements into state infrastructure standards

### Municipal Responses
- PDDUA revision accelerated with climate adaptation focus
- New flood risk mapping requirements being integrated into zoning
- Discussion of mandatory NBS elements in new developments (green roofs, permeable surfaces, retention areas)
- APP mapping completed (2024) provides regulatory basis for riparian restoration projects

## Regulatory Opportunities for NBS

1. **PDDUA revision**: Window to embed NBS requirements in updated master plan and LUOS
2. **APP restoration**: Legally mandated protection creates clear pathway for riparian NBS projects
3. **Environmental compensation**: Developer obligations (outorga onerosa) can fund NBS
4. **Federal restoration targets**: Brazil's national restoration commitments provide policy alignment
5. **Post-flood reconstruction**: Integration of NBS into infrastructure rebuilding under expedited permitting
6. **Sanitation universalization**: NBS as green infrastructure component of PMSB implementation

## Regulatory Risks and Barriers

1. **PDDUA weakening**: Risk that revised plan reduces rather than strengthens environmental provisions
2. **Licensing complexity**: Multi-level licensing (municipal, state, federal) can delay NBS projects
3. **APP encroachment**: Informal settlements in APP areas create social-environmental conflicts
4. **Post-flood shortcuts**: Emergency reconstruction may prioritize grey over green infrastructure
5. **Institutional capacity**: SMAMUS and DMAE resource constraints limit environmental enforcement
6. **Political cycles**: Municipal elections can shift priorities away from long-term NBS investments
