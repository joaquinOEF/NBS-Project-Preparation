3) Compute a “Political Readiness score” and incorporate it into overall Readiness
Add derived fields computed after questionnaire completion:

derived_political_readiness_score: number; // 0–100
derived_mandate_strength: "weak" | "moderate" | "strong";
derived_durability_risk: "low" | "medium" | "high";
Scoring rules (simple, deterministic)
Start political score at 0 and add points:
Mandate (max 40)
• Plan references:
if includes any of city_climate_plan / sectoral_plan / multi_year_investment_plan / national_or_state_plan → +20
if only "not_in_official_plan" → +0
• Endorsement:
written → +20
informal → +10
none/unknown → +0
Ownership & alignment (max 40)
• implementing_ownership:
single_department → +15
multiple_departments → +10
not_defined → +0
• internal_alignment_level:
high → +25
medium → +15
low → +0
unknown → +5
Durability (max 20)
• leadership_commitment_confidence:
high → +20
medium → +10
low → +0
unknown → +5
Risk factor penalty (subtract up to 20)
• For each risk factor selected (except "none"):
upcoming_elections → −5
land_resettlement → −7
tariff_sensitivity → −5
public_opposition → −7
• If "none" selected → no penalty
Clamp final score 0–100.
Derived labels
• mandate_strength:
strong if plan refs present AND endorsement=written
moderate if plan refs present OR endorsement=informal
weak otherwise
• durability_risk:
high if commitment=low OR alignment=low OR 2+ risk factors
medium if commitment=medium OR 1 risk factor
low if commitment=high AND alignment=high AND no risk factors
