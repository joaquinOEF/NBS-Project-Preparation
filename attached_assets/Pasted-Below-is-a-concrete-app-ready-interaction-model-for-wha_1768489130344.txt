## 3. Typology тЖТ intervention logic (the rules)

Below is a **deterministic mapping** from your typologies to intervention types.

### A. FLOOD

**Primary goal:** store, slow, redirect water

**Intervention types**

* Floodable parks / detention areas
* Sponge squares / plazas
* Wetlands / retention basins
* Permeable open spaces

### B. HEAT

**Primary goal:** reduce surface and air temperature where people are

**Intervention types**

* Tree canopy corridors
* Pocket parks
* Cool streets / shaded public realm
* Green roofs (secondary)

### C. LANDSLIDE

**Primary goal:** stabilize slopes and control runoff

**Intervention types**

* Reforestation
* Vegetated terraces
* Riparian buffers
* Controlled drainage

### D. Combined typologies (key)

Combined zones **do not get new categories** тАФ they get **hybrids**.

Example:

* `FLOOD+HEAT` тЖТ floodable parks **with canopy**
* `FLOOD+LANDSLIDE` тЖТ terraced retention + slope drainage
* `HEAT+LANDSLIDE` тЖТ reforestation + shade near settlements

## 4. OSM asset types to query (this is critical)

You should **never show all OSM features**. You pre-define **eligible asset types per intervention category**.

Below is a **clean, reusable mapping**.

---

### FLOOD interventions тЖТ OSM assets

Query only assets that can **hold or convey water**.

**OSM tags to query**

* `leisure=park`
* `leisure=playground`
* `leisure=sports_pitch`
* `landuse=grass`
* `landuse=recreation_ground`
* `natural=wetland`
* `amenity=square` (plazas)
* `amenity=school` (yards)
* `amenity=university`
* `highway=service` (for permeable retrofits, optional)

**Geometry**

* Prefer **polygons**
* Convert points (schools) to buffered areas if needed

**Why these work**

* Publicly accessible
* Typically flat
* Politically feasible
* Often already city-owned

---

### HEAT interventions тЖТ OSM assets

Query assets where **people are exposed** or where shade can be added.

**OSM tags**

* `highway=primary|secondary|tertiary`
* `amenity=square`
* `leisure=park`
* `amenity=school`
* `amenity=hospital`
* `amenity=bus_station`
* `public_transport=platform`
* `landuse=residential`

**Geometry**

* Lines (streets) тЖТ candidate corridors
* Polygons (schools, squares) тЖТ pocket shade nodes

---

### LANDSLIDE interventions тЖТ OSM assets

Query assets **on or near slopes** (filter by slope later).

**OSM tags**

* `natural=wood`
* `landuse=forest`
* `natural=scrub`
* `natural=grassland`
* `landuse=farmland`
* `waterway=stream`
* `waterway=ditch`

**Important**

* You must intersect these with **high-slope cells**
* Ignore flat parks here

## 5. Candidate scoring inside a zone (simple + explainable)

Once OSM assets are fetched inside the zone, score them.

### Generic candidate score formula

(You can reuse this everywhere with different weights.)

```
CandidateScore =
  0.4 * ZoneSuitability
+ 0.25 * ProximityToProblem
+ 0.15 * AreaPotential
+ 0.10 * OwnershipProxy
+ 0.10 * MultiBenefitBonus
```

### How to compute each term

* **ZoneSuitability**

  * Mean hazard score of the zone (flood/heat/landslide)

* **ProximityToProblem**

  * Flood zones: distance to flow accumulation lines / pooling cells
  * Heat zones: distance to high population / impervious cells
  * Landslide zones: distance to steep slopes

* **AreaPotential**

  * Based on polygon area (log-scaled)

* **OwnershipProxy**

  * * points for public assets (parks, schools, streets)

* **MultiBenefitBonus**

  * * points if asset also intersects secondary hazard

This makes the ranking feel тАЬsmartтАЭ without hidden complexity.
